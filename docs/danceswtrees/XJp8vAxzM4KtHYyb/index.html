Across all 10 real kills, three patterns hold up consistently enough to call raid-night-level findings rather than one-off noise. Overheal exceeds the Top 100 sample's single worst sampled parse on 8 of 10 kills (all but Karathress and Solarian) — a real, recurring gap, not fight-specific bad luck. Lifebloom is overweighted against Regrowth on 8 of 10 kills, with Al'ar (+17.2) and Karathress (+23.6) the largest gaps — the two exceptions are genuinely boss-driven: Void Reaver's own Top 100 average is itself Regrowth-dominant (74.8%), and Solarian saw an all-in Regrowth approach (90.1%) that crowded out Lifebloom entirely. HPM sits below the Top 100 average on 9 of 10 kills, Karathress the sole exception (8.55 vs. avg 8.07) — a consistent mana-efficiency gap that tracks the overheal pattern (mana spent on healing that doesn't count as effective). Two real anomalies worth flagging on their own: Karathress's active time (80.6%, far below the 99.2% average) is directly explained by Danceswtrees's own death mid-fight, visible in that kill's death list — not idle time in a live window. Tree of Life uptime read 0% on two kills (Morogrim, Solarian) with no death to explain either — a real, unresolved gap worth a closer look. Percentile ranged from 23rd (Kael'thas, the weakest kill by nearly every metric) to 84th (Solarian, the strongest) — a wide spread across one raid night, underscoring that per-boss variance is real and each page's findings shouldn't be assumed to generalize to the others.
