Chain Heal's two dominant ranks run inverted from the Top 100 sample on 8 of 9 kills. Vajomee casts the cheaper rank (guid 10623, 405 mana per cast, confirmed real from classResources data) as her primary (as high as 84.7% of total healing on Leotheras) while the Top 100 sample overwhelmingly favors the costlier rank instead (guid 25423, 540 mana — ~33% more per cast — as high as 87.4% there). These are two genuinely different spell ranks, not a display/localization artifact — real, distinct mana costs confirmed across every kill this raid night. Only Solarian breaks the pattern, where the costlier rank was Vajomee's own primary too. This is the single most consistent finding across the whole raid night — see each boss page's spell composition section for the exact split, and Lady Vashj's page for the full 5-rank cost ladder visible in that fight's larger sample.

Four real patterns hold up across the 9 real kills: HPS trails the Top 100 average on every single kill, no exceptions, ranging from a 492 vs. 1463 gap on Hydross to the closest showing on Void Reaver (1614 vs. 2645). Overheal exceeds the Top 100 sample's single worst parse on 6 of 9 kills (Hydross, Morogrim, Vashj, Al'ar, Void Reaver, Solarian) — a real, recurring pattern across most of the night, not one-off bad luck. Healing Stream Totem was cast on only 1 of 9 kills (Void Reaver, where its 14.4% share actually exceeded the Top 100 average of 9.3%) despite real, if modest, usage in the Top 100 sample on every single boss (1.4%-9.3%) — the fact that it appeared and outperformed on the one kill it was used rules out a talent/glyph gap as the explanation. Dark Rune was never cast on any of the 9 kills, most notably on Lady Vashj, the raid's longest fight (498s), where 52% of the Top 100 sample used one — the highest Dark Rune usage rate of any boss this raid night. Lowest-percentile priorities for a closer look: Hydross (27th) and Lady Vashj (45th), both of which also showed the lowest active time of the night (78.9% and 79.2% respectively).
