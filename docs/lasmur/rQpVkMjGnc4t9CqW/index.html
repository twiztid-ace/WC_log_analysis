A full 10-boss clear (all kills, no wipes) spanning the 19th to 72nd percentile. HPS ran below the Top 100 average on every kill (0.34x-0.67x), and overheal exceeded the sample's worst recorded parse on 8 of 10 kills - only Fathom-Lord Karathress (just under the worst-parse line) and Lady Vashj (a boss with an unusually high worst-parse ceiling) stayed in line. Circle of Healing consistently undershot the benchmark's share of the healing mix, closest on Void Reaver (79.1% vs. 81.9%) and actually exceeding it on Lady Vashj (35.5% vs. 29.8%); Renew and Prayer of Mending ran ahead of the sample on most kills to help make up the difference. Mana Potion went uncast on four kills (Hydross, Lurker, Leotheras, Morogrim) against sample usage rates of 59-81%. Kael'thas Sunstrider was both the weakest kill by percentile (19th) and by far the deadliest of the raid - 16 deaths, including one of Lasmur's own - while Morogrim Tidewalker was the strongest kill (72nd) and one of four kills with zero deaths (alongside Fathom-Lord Karathress, Void Reaver, and High Astromancer Solarian). No cooldown-timing correlation this raid points to a real missed save - Shadowfiend, Chakra, and Mana Potion are all self-targeted resource or buff tools, not defensive cooldowns for another player, even on Lady Vashj where one death landed just 1.1 seconds after a Chakra cast. Blessing of Life was never cast this raid night (0 of 10 kills). Gear was effectively consistent - the same Main Hand weapon and permanent enchant on all 10 kills, with only its temporary weapon enchant missing on two of them (Morogrim, Solarian) - and every kill had a complete consumable setup.
